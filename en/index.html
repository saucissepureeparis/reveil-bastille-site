The Bistrot

A living Parisian bistrot — the real thing, in the 4th

Le Réveil Bastille is the Parisian bistrot at its truest: tables filling up at sunrise, a young team that knows every regular by name, French cooking sent out non-stop from morning to night.

Locals come in for an espresso on Boulevard Henri IV and end up staying for lunch. Visitors stop by for a glass of wine on the terrace and find themselves at dinner hours later. The menu is honest French bistrot food — egg cocotte with foie gras, smoked salmon burger, duck confit, homemade Paris-Brest — cooked the way it should be, priced the way it used to be.

The team is young, the atmosphere is alive, the kitchen never closes. Between Bastille and the Marais, this is the place you keep coming back to.

our signature piece

A monumental bar that runs across the entire room

It's the first thing you see when you walk in: an enormous bar, sculpted like a piece of art, stretching from one end of the room to the other. A handcrafted artwork — rare in Parisian bistrots — built to bring people together, to make conversations happen over a glass of natural wine or a black coffee. This is the beating heart of Le Réveil. Worth coming in just to see it.

The Terrace

One of the largest terraces around Bastille

Sun all morning, full south at lunch, golden in the evening — our huge terrace wraps along Boulevard Henri IV. Hands down the best spot to feel Paris in the open air, right between Bastille and the Marais.

  • Breakfast on the boulevard from 7am on weekdays
  • Sunlit lunch break, steps from the Arsenal Marina
  • Aperitivo starting at 6pm — and it never really ends
  • Summer dinners under the trees until 2am
  • Sunday brunch on the terrace, well into the afternoon

The Neighbourhood

The heart of Paris 4th, between Bastille and the Marais

Le Réveil Bastille sits on one of the most coveted addresses on Boulevard Henri IV: facing the Port de l'Arsenal, two minutes from Place de la Bastille, five minutes from the Marais. The perfect neighbourhood to discover real Paris — and the perfect bistrot to stop in.

2 min walk

Place de la Bastille

The legendary square, its opera house, its history — everything in Paris starts here.

3 min walk

Port de l'Arsenal

Paris's hidden marina, right across the boulevard. Unbelievable light at sunset.

5 min walk

Le Marais

Galleries, vintage shops, hôtels particuliers — the most Parisian district of Paris is two streets away.

2 min walk

Opéra Bastille

Before or after a show, we're the natural pre-theatre stop in the area.

4 min walk

Coulée Verte René-Dumont

The original elevated park (long before NYC's High Line), starting just steps away.

Île Saint-Louis

Postcard Paris, a few minutes' stroll across the Seine from the bistrot.
